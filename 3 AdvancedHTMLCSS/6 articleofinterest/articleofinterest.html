The Cheerleading World

All-Star Cheer:

All-Star cheer is a club type of cheer, doesn't have anything to do with that school you go to. There are thousands of all-star cheer gyms across the world that compete from November-May. The cheer season starts in June and they practice all summer, then start competing in November. For all-star cheer, each team has one routine that they are working to prefect so they can be the best. Their routine is to a custom made music mix for their team. There are also multiple different divisions. There is level 1-7 and age groups tiny to open. Open means you can be any age that's over 18. Something else that makes the divisions is the teams have sizes. There is x-small to large teams. Most all-star teams are working towards the same goal which is to win their championship at the ends of the season. For levels 1-5, their championship is called The Summit and takes place in Florida. For levels 6 and 7, their championship is called The Cheerleading Worlds and also takes place in Florida. This is a very hard but fun sport.

Michigan High School Cheer:

Michigan high school cheer is different then all the other states. There is a sideline season where they cheer on the sidelines at football games and a competitive season where they comoete every weekend trying to get the highest score. Michigan HS cheer is based off of technique and cleanliness. Each team has 3 rounds that they compete every weekend. Michigan cheer does their rounds to words. They start competing in December and depending how far in playoffs they get, can compete all the way to March. There is division 1-4 varsity and jv teams. The varsity teams are all working towards making it to MHSAA State Finals the first weekend of March. Only the top 8 teams from each division make it to states. This is a very competitive and amazing sport.

Every Other State High School Cheer:

Unlike Michigan, all other states for high school cheer are going to go to UCA in Florida at the end of their competitive season. They also have a sideline season where they cheer on the sidelines of the football games for their school. The divisions are based on school size and there are varsity and jv teams. Only the varsity teams compete at the end of the season. These temas don't compete every weekend like the other types of cheer. They work all season to just compete once at the national competition. These high school teams have two routines that they compete. They have a game day routine which is when they use poms and sign and cheer for their school using words. The other routine is to music and it's where they show all of their hardest stunts and tumbling together in one routine. This sport is filled with very talented athletes.
