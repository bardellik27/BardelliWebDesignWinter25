The Cheerleading World

All-Star Cheer:

All-Star cheer is a club type of cheer, doesn't have anything to do with that school you go to. There are thousands of all-star cheer gyms across the world that compete from November-May. The cheer season starts in June and they practice all summer, then start competing in November. For all-star cheer, each team has one routine that they are working to prefect so they can be the best. There are also multiple different divisions. There is level 1-7 and age groups tiny to open. Open means you can be any age that's over 18. Something else that makes the divisions is the teams have sizes. There is x-small to large teams. Most all-star teams are working towards the same goal which is to win their championship at the ends of the season. For levels 1-5, their championship is called The Summit and takes place in Florida. For levels 6 and 7, their championship is called The Cheerleading Worlds and also takes place in Florida. This is a very hard but fun sport.

Michigan High School Cheer:

Michigan high school cheer is different then all the other states. Michigan HS cheer is based off of technique and cleanliness. Each team has 3 rounds that they compete every weekend. They start competing in December and depending how far in playoffs they get, can compete all the way to March. There is division 1-4 varsity and jv teams. The varsity teams are all working towards making it to MHSAA State Finals the first weekend of March. Only the top 8 teams from each division make it to states. This is a very competitive sport.
