Technology Adoption


What is technology adoption?

Technology adoption refers to the process of integrating and using new technologies within an organization or individual's life. It involves understanding the benefits, challenges, and strategies for successfully implementing and adopting new technologies. The process can be understood through the Technology Adoption Curve, which categorizes users into groups based on their willingness to embrace new technologies

Technology Adoption Curve

This model helps predict how a technology will spread through a population. It identifies five adopter groups:

  • Innovators: The first to adopt, risk-takers who enjoy being early with new technologies
  • Early Adopters: Visionary leaders who understand the potential of new technologies and influence others.
  • Early Majority: Pragmatic users who adopt after seeing the benefits and endorsement from early adopters.
  • Late Majority: Skeptical adopters who adopt only when the technology is widely accepted and industry standard
  • Laggards: The most resistant group, often hesitant to change
